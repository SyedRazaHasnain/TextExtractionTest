Section 1: Data Protection and Privacy Requirements

Organizations must implement comprehensive data protection measures in accordance with this regulation. Personal data, as defined in Article 4(1), must be processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently. Organizations shall:
a) Implement end-to-end encryption for all personal data during transmission and storage, using AES-256 or equivalent standards.
b) Conduct mandatory Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) for high-risk processing activities every 6 months.
c) Maintain detailed processing logs for a minimum period of 5 years.
d) Appoint a qualified Data Protection Officer (DPO) when processing operations exceed 10,000 records annually.

Section 2: Security and Breach Notification Protocols

2.1 Security Measures
Organizations must maintain robust security protocols, including:
- Multi-factor authentication for all system access
- Regular security audits at intervals not exceeding 3 months
- Automated vulnerability scanning on a weekly basis
- Annual penetration testing by certified third-party providers

2.2 Breach Notification
In the event of a data breach, organizations must:
a) Notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours of breach detection
b) Inform affected individuals within 7 days if the breach poses a high risk
c) Document all breach incidents and remediation measures
d) Implement corrective actions within 30 days of the incident

Section 3: Operational Compliance and Reporting

3.1 Documentation Requirements
Organizations shall maintain and regularly update the following documentation:
- Data processing inventory (reviewed quarterly)
- Written security procedures
- Employee training records
- Vendor compliance assessments

3.2 Reporting Obligations
Organizations must submit:
a) Annual compliance reports by March 31st of each year
b) Quarterly processing statistics to the regulatory authority
c) Immediate notification of any significant changes to processing activities
d) Updated DPO contact information within 10 business days of any change

Section 4: Technical Infrastructure Requirements

4.1 System Requirements
All systems processing regulated data must:
- Maintain 99.9% uptime for critical services
- Implement automated backup systems with 15-minute recovery point objectives
- Support data portability in machine-readable formats
- Maintain separate development, testing, and production environments

4.2 Monitoring and Logging
Organizations must implement:
a) Real-time monitoring of all system access attempts
b) Automated alerts for suspicious activities
c) Immutable audit logs retained for 7 years
d) Monthly system performance reports

Section 5: Penalties and Enforcement

5.1 Violations and Fines
Non-compliance penalties shall be assessed as follows:
- Critical violations: Up to €20 million or 4% of global revenue
- Major violations: Up to €10 million or 2% of global revenue
- Minor violations: Up to €5 million or 1% of global revenue

5.2 Enforcement Actions
The regulatory authority may:
a) Conduct unannounced audits
b) Issue cease and desist orders
c) Require mandatory third-party assessments
d) Publish violation notices publicly
